Cancer is a disease caused by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body. It can develop in any part of the body and can lead to serious health problems or even death. According to the World Health Organization, cancer is the second leading cause of death worldwide, responsible for an estimated 10 million deaths in 2020.

The most common types of cancer worldwide are lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, and stomach cancers. These types of cancer have a high incidence rate and cause significant morbidity and mortality. However, the incidence and mortality rates of cancer can vary depending on factors such as age, gender, lifestyle habits, and geographic location.
